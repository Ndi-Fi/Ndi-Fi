# Ndi-Fi: Tokenomics & Economic Analysis

## 1. The NdiPoint (NDI) Token

The NdiPoint (NDI) token is the central utility and rewards token of the Ndi-Fi ecosystem. Its design is crucial for incentivizing participation, distributing value, and potentially facilitating governance.

### 1.1. Token Supply & Distribution

- **Max Supply:** `1,000,000,000` NDI (1 billion). This is a hard cap enforced in the `NdiPoint` contract, meaning the total supply can never exceed this amount.
- **Initial Supply:** `100,000,000` NDI (100 million). This supply is minted to the initial owner at the time of deployment.
- **Inflation Mechanism:** The remaining `900,000,000` NDI are reserved for future distribution. New tokens can only be created by an address holding the `MINTER_ROLE` or `REWARD_DISTRIBUTOR_ROLE`. This is the protocol's primary mechanism for inflation, which is intended to be used for rewarding users and growing the ecosystem.

### 1.2. Token Utility

The primary utility of the NDI token, as defined in the contracts, is for **rewards and incentives**. The `NdiPointIntegrationExample.sol` contract demonstrates how NDI can be distributed as rewards for various activities:

- **Staking Rewards:** Users who stake assets can earn NDI.
- **Lending & Borrowing Incentives:** Users who participate in the lending market can be rewarded with NDI for both supplying and borrowing assets.

While not explicitly implemented, the token is well-positioned for future use in **governance**, where NDI holders could vote on key economic parameters.

## 2. Economic Mechanisms & Value Flow

The Ndi-Fi economy is designed as a flywheel, where protocol activity generates revenue, which in turn funds rewards and incentivizes more activity.

### 2.1. Revenue Generation

The protocol generates revenue from two primary sources:

1.  **Lending Origination Fees (`originationFeeRate`):** Borrowers in the `NdiFiLending` contract are charged a one-time fee when they take out a loan. This fee is a percentage of the loan principal and is a direct source of revenue.
2.  **Vault Yield (`skimProfits`):** The `NdiFiVault` generates yield by lending its assets. Any yield generated by the vault that is above and beyond what is owed to its depositors (including stakers) is considered protocol profit. The `skimProfits` function in the `NdiFiStaking` contract allows the owner to capture this excess yield.

### 2.2. Value Distribution

Value is distributed to the following participants:

- **Vault Depositors / Stakers:** Earn yield on their assets that are deposited in the `NdiFiVault`. The `NdiFiStaking` contract specifies a target `apy` for stakers.
- **Ecosystem Participants:** Receive NDI token rewards for performing desired actions like staking, lending, and borrowing.
- **The Protocol:** Accrues value through origination fees and excess vault yield. This revenue can be used to fund further development, buy back and burn NDI tokens, or be distributed to token holders.

## 3. Key Economic Levers (Governance Parameters)

The health and growth of the Ndi-Fi economy are controlled by several key parameters that can be adjusted by the protocol's administrators (and eventually, by governance). These levers allow the protocol to adapt to changing market conditions.

### In `NdiFiLending`:

- **`collateralFactor`:** Determines the maximum amount a user can borrow against their collateral. A higher factor increases capital efficiency but also increases protocol risk.
- **`liquidationThreshold`:** The collateral-to-debt ratio at which a loan is eligible for liquidation. This is a critical risk parameter.
- **`originationFeeRate`:** The fee charged to borrowers. A key lever for controlling protocol revenue.
- **`liquidationPenalty`:** The bonus paid to liquidators. This incentivizes third parties to liquidate risky loans, securing the protocol.

### In `NdiFiStaking`:

- **`apy`:** The target annual yield for stakers. This is a primary lever for incentivizing users to stake assets.
- **`minStake` / `maxStake`:** Controls the barrier to entry and concentration of staking.
- **`WITHDRAW_WAIT`:** The 21-day lock-up period for withdrawals. This is a powerful tool for ensuring protocol stability and reducing capital flight during volatile periods.

### In `NdiFiVault`:

- **`stakingCap`:** The maximum amount of assets that can be deposited into the vault. This acts as a ceiling on the protocol's total value locked (TVL) and overall risk exposure.

## 4. Economic Flywheel

The Ndi-Fi economy is designed to be self-reinforcing:

1.  **Incentives:** The protocol offers a competitive `apy` and NDI token rewards to attract stakers and liquidity providers.
2.  **Liquidity Growth:** Users deposit assets into the `NdiFiVault` to earn yield, increasing the protocol's TVL.
3.  **Lending Activity:** The deep liquidity in the vault allows the `NdiFiLending` contract to offer loans to borrowers.
4.  **Revenue Generation:** Borrowers pay origination fees, and the vault generates yield, creating revenue for the protocol.
5.  **Value Accrual:** This revenue can be used to increase rewards for stakers or be used in other ways (like buybacks) that add value to the NDI token.
6.  **Increased Demand:** As the protocol grows and generates more revenue, the attractiveness of its yield and the value of its NDI rewards increase, creating more demand and starting the cycle anew.
